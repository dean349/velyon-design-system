## 5. Capital Gains Tax Implications

### Spanish IRNR (CGT) Position

Under Spanish tax law, AEAT taxes the **total consideration received** for the property disposal — €315,000. AEAT does **not** separately strip out the chattel/furniture value from the land and property value for IRNR purposes when the sale is structured as a single unallocated price.

**Key points:**
- AEAT assesses gain on the full €315,000 escritura value. The furniture is included in that figure — it does not reduce the taxable gain.
- The original CGT computation (taxable gain ~€109,900; tax ~€20,881) **already correctly reflects this** — the €315,000 is used as the full disposal proceeds.
- There is **no Spanish mechanism** for a seller to allocate a portion of the sale price to chattels and thereby reduce the property disposal gain, because the Modelo 210/IRNR is charged on the gross disposición proceeds.
