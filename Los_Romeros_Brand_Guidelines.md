# Los Romeros Limited — Brand Guidelines

*Last updated: April 10, 2026*

## 1. Brand Positioning & Story

**One-Liner:**
Delivering unyielding clarity and forensic accountability across cross-border legal compliance.

**What it does:**
Los Romeros Limited acts as a corporate entity anchored in transparent, evidence-based reasoning. Initially structured as a property holding vehicle spanning UK and Spanish jurisdictions, the brand now stands as a vehicle for regulatory scrutiny, legal exposure analysis, and rigorous financial audit against substandard professional advice.

**Positioning Strategy:**
The brand positions itself not as a commercial service trying to sell, but as an authoritative dossier presenting irrefutable facts. It sits on the "shelf" of legal compliance, forensic accounting, and regulatory enforcement.

## 2. Tone of Voice

**Serious & Resolute:**
There is no room for ambiguity or softening of language. Tone must convey the gravity of legal and financial exposure. Claims must be direct, avoiding hyperbolic marketing speak. 

**Factual & Evidentiary:**
Every statement is treated as a fact backed by a verifiable source. We do not use "we think" or "maybe." We use "forensic analysis demonstrates," "the statute dictates," and "the liability stands at."

**Precise & Structural:**
Information must be layered logically, mirroring legal briefs or forensic accounting reports. Utilize clear headings, bullet points, and high-level summaries leading into granular detail.

**Personality Trait Summary:**
- Authoritative
- Forensic
- Unyielding
- Transparent

## 3. Messaging Framework

**Core Value Themes:**
1. **Regulatory Accountability:** Professional networks must be held to statutory standards; negligence has quantifiable consequences.
2. **Cross-Border Transparency:** Legal structures spanning the UK and Spain require granular, disclosed oversight of both jurisdictions' anti-avoidance laws.
3. **Evidence-Based Disclosure:** Complete, unredacted financial truths over opaque "16% saving" assurances.

**Primary Pitch / Objective:**
To document, pursue, and rectify instances of professional breach of duty, focusing sharply on undisclosed risks (e.g., Article 108 LMV) and miscalculated corporate exit tax cascades.

## 4. Customer / Audience Language

Because this brand addresses regulators (ICO, ICAEW, FCA, AEAT), legal counterparts, and forensic accountants, the language must align with professional statutory contexts.

### Words/Phrases to Use:
- Forensic analysis
- Material statutory risk
- Regulatory reporting
- Duty of care (Hedley Byrne, Caparo)
- Civil / Regulatory / Criminal violations
- Sede Electrónica 
- Liability exposure
- Compliance gap

### Words/Phrases to Avoid:
- "Synergy," "Holistic," or typical corporate buzzwords
- Emotional appeals (e.g., "tragedy," "sad situation") — stick to "financial harm" or "breach of duty."
- Vague estimations. Always use specific currency amounts or percentages.

## 5. Writing Mechanics & Formatting

- Use explicit active voice: *“Wincham failed to disclose the exit tax costs.”* (Not *“The exit tax costs were not disclosed by Wincham.”*)
- Always italicize legal citations (*Hedley Byrne & Co Ltd v Heller & Partners Ltd [1964] AC 465*).
- Break dense information into scannable lists and use bold text for emphasis on key regulatory bodies or statutes.
