## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Wincham International Limited charged Los Romeros Limited a total of **£9,600** in fees for services connected to the sale of a property in Lanzarote, Spain at €315,000. A forensic comparison against verified independent market providers demonstrates that the **same services, sourced independently, would have cost between £2,678.63 and £4,553.63** — including all Spanish notary fees.

This means Wincham's £9,600 bill was **more than double the maximum expected market rate**. The overcharge equates to between **£5,046.37 and £6,921.37 in excess fees**, representing a markup of between **111% and 258% above fair market value**.

Furthermore, the billing structure itself is legally irregular: fees for Spanish-jurisdiction services were directed to a UK entity's bank account, with no separate UK agreement produced for the UK-jurisdiction portion of the work.

## SECTION 2: WHAT THE CONTRACT SAID WAS INCLUDED

The Letter of Engagement was signed between Los Romeros Limited and **Wincham Spanish Services SL** (the Spanish entity). The £5,000 + VAT (£6,000) fee was stated to cover:

**Included services (£6,000 legal fee):**
- Acting as independent Spanish Lawyers & Tax Advisors for the sale
- Liaising with agents and supplying documents to buyer's lawyer for due diligence
- Producing, reviewing, and approving the contract of sale for signature
- Legal, tax, and regulatory compliance, including registering the sale at the Spanish Land Registry
- Coordinating with the Spanish Notary on all completion documentation
- Producing a Completion Statement with all retentions detailed
- Requesting proof of Plusvalia Tax and 3% CGT retention payments from buyer's lawyer
- Reclaiming any 3% CGT retention from AEAT if applicable
- Collecting sale proceeds into client bank account with no bank charges
- Advising on tax-efficient withdrawal of final funds from the UK company

**The contract explicitly stated** that the £5,000 fee *"relates solely to work carried out in Spain and under its jurisdiction,"* and that any UK-based work *"will be covered by a separate Agreement if applicable."*

**Expressly excluded from the £6,000 fee:**
- Costs for Powers of Attorney and related translations
- Actual Spanish tax payments (Plusvalia, 3% CGT retention)
- Spanish Notary costs
- Costs for preparing a new Certificate of Good Standing or new company documents

**The £3,600 component** was required specifically for UK Company Documents prepared in English and Spanish for the Spanish Notary — yet **no separate UK agreement was ever produced**, as the contract required. This fee was bundled with the Spanish fee and paid to the same UK account.

## SECTION 5: THE BILLING IRREGULARITY — CROSS-BORDER PAYMENT SCHEME

Beyond the gross overcharging, the billing structure itself contains serious legal irregularities:

### 5.1 UK Entity Receiving Spanish Contract Fees
- The contractual party was **Wincham Spanish Services SL** (Spain)
- Payment was directed to **Wincham International Limited** (UK) — a completely separate legal entity
- This was mandated by Mark Roach, Managing Director: *"payment can only be made from another UK Sterling Account"*
- The sources describe this as a **"suspicious billing structure"** flagging **"significant legal irregularities that may violate both British and Spanish law"**

### 5.2 No Separate UK Agreement for UK Work
- The Spanish Letter of Engagement explicitly required a *"separate Agreement"* for any UK-based work
- The £3,600 document preparation fee was explicitly UK-jurisdiction work
- **No separate UK agreement was ever produced or signed** — the fee was simply bundled with the Spanish fee and invoiced to the same UK account

### 5.3 Fees Classified as "Off-Statement Disbursements — UK Jurisdiction"
- The Completion Statement itself classifies the £9,600 as *"OFF-STATEMENT DISBURSEMENTS (UK JURISDICTION)"*
- This means even Wincham's own documentation acknowledges these were UK-jurisdiction charges — yet they were billed under a Spanish contract

## SECTION 6: LEGAL ASSESSMENT

### 6.1 Applicable Legal Claims

**In UK Courts:**
- **Unjust enrichment** — Wincham International Limited (UK) received £9,600 for services contracted under Spanish law with Wincham Spanish Services SL (Spain)
- **Fraudulent misrepresentation** — the client was told to pay a Spanish contract to a UK entity with no legal basis
- **Breach of contract** — no separate UK agreement was produced for the UK portion of work as the contract required

**In Spanish Courts:**
- Section 6.3 of the Letter of Engagement explicitly preserves claims for *"fraudulent misrepresentation and/or fraudulent concealment prior to the execution of this Letter"* — the Section 6.3 client waiver does **not** extinguish this avenue
- Spanish consumer protection law — Real Decreto Legislativo 1/2007 — may apply given the egregious fee markup

**Regulatory Referrals:**
- **ICAEW** — as Wincham operates as accountants, the billing practices constitute a professional conduct matter
